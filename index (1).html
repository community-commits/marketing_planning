01

Why should vets care? Clinical

The clinical need drives everything. Capture the real language used in this room — it's what all copy runs off.

15 min
?What's the core clinical problem this workshop solves? What are vets currently getting wrong or lacking confidence in?
?What does the vet feel when this type of case walks into their consult room? What's the fear or gap in that moment?
?What does the vet want for their patient — and for themselves professionally — after doing this workshop?
?The Monday morning test — what specific thing can they do confidently on Monday that they couldn't on Friday?
?What is the TOTAL real cost of attending — and what is the full ROI argument that beats it?
?Any clinical trends or industry shifts that make this topic especially relevant right now?
02

Workshop highlights Clinical

What makes this workshop worth attending? At 9 months out the full curriculum may not be locked — capture what's known and flag gaps to follow up with the facilitator separately.

10 min
?What are the skills and capabilities attendees will leave with? Focus on what they can DO, not just what they'll have covered.
?Format — hands-on cadaver? Live animal? Theory:practical ratio? Cohort size? What's the hands-on experience like?
?Facilitator credibility — why should vets trust this specific person to teach this topic?
?CPD hours and any accreditation angle?
?Venue and experience — what's worth saying about the location, facilities, networking, or social side?
?What's genuinely different about VetPrac's delivery vs a conference session or online course on the same topic?
?Is anyone else running a similar workshop — AVA, universities, private providers? How does VetPrac compare?
?Promotional video — what needs to be in it? (Under 90 seconds — this is not a curriculum recap)
03

Audience clarity Strategy

Get precise about who this is for and who it isn't. Specific targeting makes marketing sharper — and helps vets self-select in or out before they register.

10 min
?Who is this workshop FOR? Experience level, what they should already be comfortable doing, practice type, typical caseload.
?Who is this workshop NOT for? Be honest — this protects the room quality and prevents wrong-fit registrations.
